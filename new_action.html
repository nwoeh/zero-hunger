Take Action

You don't have to solve the whole problem. Just do something.

Individual actions add up. Here are practical ways to fight hunger — starting today.

At home

Reduce your own food waste.

01

Plan your meals

The average household throws away 30–40% of the food it buys. Shopping with a list and planning meals for the week cuts waste significantly.

02

Ignore "best before" dates

"Best before" means peak quality, not safety. Most food is fine to eat days or weeks after this date. Trust your senses — smell it, look at it.

03

Use your freezer

Freezing food before it goes bad is one of the easiest ways to cut household waste. Bread, meat, leftovers, and most vegetables freeze well.

In your community

Support people close to home.

04

Donate to a food bank

Local food banks need non-perishables year-round, not just during the holidays. Canned proteins, pasta, and cooking oil are always in demand.

05

Volunteer your time

Food banks, soup kitchens, and community gardens always need volunteers. Even a few hours a month makes a real difference for people in your area.

06

Support SNAP

SNAP (food stamps) is one of the most effective hunger-fighting programs in the US. Contact your representatives to protect and expand it.

Bigger picture

Use your voice and your wallet.

07

Donate to effective orgs

Organizations like the World Food Programme, Action Against Hunger, and Feeding America have strong track records. Even small donations go far.

08

Contact your representatives

Call or email your local and national representatives about food policy. Politicians respond to constituent pressure — especially when many people do it.

09

Spread the word

Share this site. Talk about hunger with people you know. Awareness is the first step — most people don't know how bad the problem is or how solvable it is.
