What Needs to Change

Six concrete actions the UN can take now.

These aren't radical proposals — they are pragmatic, evidence-based steps already proven in parts of the world.

01

Mandatory donation laws

Require large retailers to donate unsold food rather than destroy it — following France, the first country to ban supermarkets from discarding edible food.

02

Fiscal incentives for redistribution

Tax exemptions and waste-fee discounts for businesses that redirect surplus food to charities — modeled on Italy's "Last Minute Market" program.

03

Standardized date labeling

Harmonize "best before" and "use by" labels globally to stop billions of pounds of safe, edible food from being thrown away due to consumer confusion.

04

Infrastructure investment

Fund local storage facilities and rural road and rail networks in low-income nations — preventing post-harvest losses that currently reach up to 60%.

05

Binding accountability mechanisms

Tie trade agreements and development aid to measurable hunger-reduction progress, with public ranking systems and transparent reporting.

06

A coordinated global hunger fund

Replace fragmented small programs with a pooled global fund that deploys resources strategically at the scale the crisis demands.
